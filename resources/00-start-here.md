## What is the Swift Student Challenge?

Apple's annual competition open to students worldwide. You submit a Swift Playground app (`.swiftpm`) that runs on iPad or Mac. Judges evaluate creativity, technical execution, and the story behind your app.

- **Official page:** https://developer.apple.com/swift-student-challenge/
- **Eligibility:** Enrolled in an accredited school or university, or graduated within the past 6 months
- **Format:** Swift Playgrounds app (`.swiftpm`) — not an Xcode project
- **Typical window:** Applications open in February, close ~3 weeks later

---

## Recommended learning path

### 4–8 weeks out
1. Pick one idea and build something small every day
2. Work through [02-swiftui.md](02-swiftui.md) — Stanford CS193p is the gold standard
3. Bookmark [09-submission-storytelling.md](09-submission-storytelling.md) early — the story matters as much as the code

### 2–3 weeks out
1. Lock in your idea — simple, focused, and polished beats ambitious and broken
2. Add accessibility ([08-design-accessibility.md](08-design-accessibility.md)) — judges actively look for it
3. Write a few tests ([07-testing.md](07-testing.md)) — it signals craft

### Final week
1. Record a short demo video (many applicants skip this — don't)
2. Write your description using the storytelling template in [09-submission-storytelling.md](09-submission-storytelling.md)
3. Test on a real device, not just the simulator

---

## What judges look for

Based on winner write-ups and past scholarship advice:

| Factor | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| **Creativity** | Original idea or a fresh take on a familiar problem |
| **Technical execution** | SwiftUI, concurrency, and accessibility used correctly |
| **Design** | Follows Human Interface Guidelines, feels native |
| **Accessibility** | VoiceOver labels, Dynamic Type support, Reduce Motion |
| **Story** | Clear problem → solution → impact in your written description |

---

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Submitting an Xcode project instead of a `.swiftpm` Playground app
- Over-scoping — a polished small app beats a broken large one
- Skipping accessibility — it is explicitly evaluated
- Writing a generic description ("I made an app that...") instead of a personal story
- Not recording a demo video
