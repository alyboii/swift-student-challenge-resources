## Storytelling Template

Use this as a skeleton for your written description:

**1. The problem** _(1–2 sentences)_
> What frustration, gap, or challenge does your app address? Make it personal — why did this matter to you?

**2. Your solution** _(2–3 sentences)_
> What does your playground do? What's the core interaction? Describe it as if you're showing it to a friend, not a specification.

**3. Why it's different** _(1–2 sentences)_
> What makes your approach creative, inclusive, or impactful? Avoid generic claims — be specific.

**4. The demo flow** _(2–3 sentences)_
> Walk through what a user experiences in the first 60 seconds. What do they see, tap, and feel?

**5. Technical highlights** _(bullet list)_
> - SwiftUI: [specific use — animation, custom layout, etc.]
> - Concurrency: [what runs async and why]
> - Accessibility: [VoiceOver, Dynamic Type, Reduce Motion support]
> - Testing: [what you tested and why those cases matter]

## Common Description Mistakes

| Weak | Strong |
|---|---|
| "I built a to-do app" | "I built this after watching my sister lose track of her homework every week" |
| "It uses SwiftUI and animations" | "The animation gives immediate feedback when a habit is completed — it was the most important UX decision I made" |
| "It is accessible" | "Every element has VoiceOver labels tested with a screen reader; animations respect Reduce Motion" |
| "This was a fun project" | "This is a problem I live with — building the app changed how I approach it every day" |
