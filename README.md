## Why this repo?

Every year thousands of students compete for Apple's Swift Student Challenge — and most of them waste weeks hunting for the right tutorial, the right WWDC session, the right storytelling angle.

This is the list. One link. Everything vetted.

It started as the preparation notes for a single submission (2025), but is designed to grow into a **shared knowledge base** for every SSC applicant. If it helped you, star it and send the link to the next person preparing.

> **Note:** Some resources and notes are written in Turkish. All links and titles are in English. Global contributions are very welcome — see [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md).

## How to Use

**If you're just starting (4–8 weeks out):**
1. Read [00-start-here.md](resources/00-start-here.md) for the roadmap
2. Pick up [02-swiftui.md](resources/02-swiftui.md) if you're new to SwiftUI — Stanford CS193p is the gold standard
3. Bookmark [09-submission-storytelling.md](resources/09-submission-storytelling.md) early — the story is half the score

**If you're in the final stretch (1–2 weeks out):**
1. Go straight to [09-submission-storytelling.md](resources/09-submission-storytelling.md)
2. Check [08-design-accessibility.md](resources/08-design-accessibility.md) — judges care about accessibility
3. Review your concurrency and testing checklist

**Resource levels:**
- `B` — Beginner (new to Swift / iOS)
- `I` — Intermediate (knows basics, building real apps)
- `A` — Advanced (architecture, performance, advanced APIs)

## Contributing

This library grows through contributions. Adding a resource takes 5 minutes.

See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) for the full guide.

**Quick version:**
1. Open the relevant file in `resources/`
2. Add your entry in this format:
   ```
   - **[Title](URL)** — Type: `Video/Doc/Article/Repo/Tool` | Topic: `SwiftUI/Concurrency/...` | Level: `B/I/A`
     - Notes: why it's useful, what makes it different
   ```
3. Open a PR

Contributions in any language are welcome as long as the title and link are clear. Annotate non-English resources with a `(TR)`, `(JA)`, `(ES)` etc. tag.
